History of DNA

Deoxyribonucliec Acid(DNA) was discovered by a Swiss biologist called Johann Friedrich Miescher. It was first isolated by him in the year 1869. He was carrying out experiments using salt solution to understand what made up a white blood cell when he noticed that when he added acid to a solution of cells, a substance seprated from the solution and this substance dissolved again when alkali was added. He also noticed that this substance had different properties from the protiens which he had studied.
He called the substance "nuclein" because it came from the cell nuclei.

Isolation of the 5 nucleotide

In 1881, Albrecht Kossel, a German biochemist, who studied and gained a better understnding of this substance called it a "nucleic acid" and gave a chemical name as Deoxyribonucliec(DNA). He also isolated the five nucleotide bases that are the building blocks of DNA and RNA which are; Adenine, Cytosine, Guanine, Thymine and Uracil
